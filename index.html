Featured Project — FMCG • Process Improvement • Requirements Analysis

Online Canteen Ordering System
Unilever PLC

Unilever's 1,500-employee UK office was losing an estimated 35 minutes per employee per lunch break to canteen queues — with just 10–15 minutes left to eat. I conducted a full business analysis engagement to design a digital ordering solution, covering everything from the initial business case through to functional specifications and a complete RACI-governed project plan.

4 Key Deliverables
  • Business Case with ROI Projection — Documented a £200k total investment with a full cost-benefit analysis across Years 0–6, identifying Year 5 as the break-even point (£10k positive return) and projecting a 45% reduction in food waste as the primary non-financial benefit.
  • Stakeholder Analysis & RACI Matrix — Produced a 12-stakeholder engagement register (Employees, Exec Management, Canteen Manager, Chef, Payroll, IT, HR, Vendors and more), mapping Power/Interest quadrants, communication channels and engagement strategies — paired with a 9-activity RACI matrix governing all project phases from analysis through to delivery.
  • Functional Requirements & Use Case Diagram — Captured front-end features for two user groups (Employees: login, menu browse, cart, payment, order tracking; Canteen Staff: order view, status updates, menu management) and backend admin requirements including inventory management, GDPR-compliant data handling, role-based access control and integration APIs.
  • Options Analysis & Risk Register — Evaluated three strategic options (Do Nothing, Expand Canteen Capacity, Implement Online System), recommended the digital solution, and documented 9 project risks including employee resistance, system downtime, data security concerns and integration challenges — each with mitigation considerations.

Healthcare — Patient Experience • Operational Improvement

Patient Experience Improvement
Barnet Hospital

Barnet Hospital, a leading multi-specialty teaching hospital in Central London, faced mounting patient complaints and operational inefficiencies. I was engaged to analyse the root causes, identify key improvement areas and produce a structured business analysis report with an actionable recommendations dashboard.

4 Key Deliverables
  • Root Cause Analysis Report — Systematically categorised patient complaint themes (wait times, communication failures, resource bottlenecks) using fishbone / Ishikawa analysis to identify the underlying operational drivers behind declining patient satisfaction scores.
  • Stakeholder Impact & Communication Plan — Mapped key hospital stakeholders (clinical staff, ward managers, administration, patients, NHS Trust leadership) by influence and impact, and produced a structured communication and engagement strategy to align all parties around the improvement initiative.
  • Resource Allocation Review & Gap Analysis — Analysed current staffing and bed capacity against patient flow demand data, identifying peak bottleneck windows and under-resourced departments — providing the evidence base for targeted resource reallocation recommendations.
  • Performance Dashboard & KPI Framework — Designed a visual analytics dashboard (evidenced by the project output file) tracking the hospital's key patient experience metrics — including average wait time, complaint resolution rate and patient satisfaction score — to enable ongoing monitoring of improvement progress.

Electronics Retail — SQL • Database Design • Inventory Intelligence

Sales & Inventory Intelligence
Tech Haven Electronics

Tech Haven Electronics needed to move beyond manual reporting and unlock the intelligence held within their transactional data. Working directly with the raw SQL file, I designed and populated a relational database, then built a structured set of queries to surface customer-level purchasing intelligence, dormant inventory and product-level sales performance.

4 Key Deliverables
  • Relational Database Schema Design & Population — Designed and implemented a normalised relational database structure (confirmed as a `.sql` file deliverable) to store and organise Tech Haven's customer, product, sales and transaction data — establishing a clean, query-ready data foundation for all subsequent analysis.
  • Customer Purchase History Query Set — Developed SQL queries to extract individual customer purchase records, joining customer detail, product and transaction tables to produce a complete per-customer sales history — enabling the business to understand buying behaviour, identify VIP customers and personalise marketing outreach.
  • Zero-Sales Product Identification Report — Wrote a targeted SQL query to identify all products within the inventory that had never been purchased, producing a prioritised report of dormant stock — equipping the merchandising team with the evidence needed to rationalise inventory, reduce holding costs and adjust purchasing decisions.
  • Product & Revenue Performance Analysis — Built SQL-based reporting queries to aggregate sales volume, total revenue and average selling price by product and category — delivering a ranked performance view that highlighted Tech Haven's top revenue drivers, underperforming lines and pricing optimisation opportunities.

Process Mapping — AS-IS Documentation • Lucidchart • BPMN

Travel Expense Reimbursement
AS-IS Process Mapping

This process analysis project focused on documenting the complete current-state (AS-IS) workflow for employee travel expense reimbursement. Using Lucidchart, I created a structured swimlane process map (confirmed as a `.png` visual output) that clearly illustrated every step, decision point and handoff — exposing friction in the approval chain and forming the baseline for future process redesign.

4 Key Deliverables
  • AS-IS Swimlane Process Map (Lucidchart) — Produced a detailed, publication-ready swimlane diagram in Lucidchart (exported as the project's `.png` output), mapping the complete travel expense reimbursement process across all participating roles — Employee, Line Manager, Finance Team and Payroll — with every step, decision gateway and data flow clearly labelled using BPMN notation.
  • Process Narrative & Step-by-Step Documentation — Accompanied the visual map with a written process narrative describing each stage of the reimbursement journey in plain language — capturing the trigger event (trip completion), the submission method, approval routing logic, finance review criteria and final payroll processing steps for use in training and handover documentation.
  • Pain Point Register & Friction Analysis — Identified and documented the key process inefficiencies observed in the AS-IS state — including ambiguous approval authority, manual form submission steps, lack of submission status visibility for employees and inconsistent processing timescales between departments — ranked by frequency of occurrence and business impact.
  • Baseline & Scope Definition for TO-BE Redesign — Formalised the AS-IS documentation as the analytical baseline for a future process improvement phase, clearly defining the in-scope process boundaries, out-of-scope exclusions, the process owner and the specific pain points that the TO-BE redesign must address — ensuring the improvement project starts from a shared, evidence-based understanding of the current state.
