Featured Project — FMCG • Process Improvement • Requirements Analysis

Online Canteen Ordering System
Unilever PLC

Unilever's 1,500-employee UK office was losing an estimated 35 minutes per employee per lunch break to canteen queues. I conducted a full business analysis engagement — covering the business case, stakeholder mapping, functional specifications and a RACI-governed project plan — to design a digital ordering solution.

4 Key Deliverables
  • Business Case with ROI Projection Documented a £200k total investment with a full cost-benefit analysis across Years 0–6, identifying Year 5 as the break-even point and projecting a 45% reduction in food waste as the primary non-financial benefit.
  • Stakeholder Analysis & RACI Matrix Produced a 12-stakeholder engagement register mapping Power/Interest quadrants and communication strategies, paired with a 9-activity RACI matrix governing all phases from analysis through to delivery.
  • Functional Requirements & Use Case Diagram Captured front-end features for two user groups (Employees and Canteen Staff) plus backend admin requirements including inventory management, GDPR-compliant data handling, role-based access control and integration APIs.
  • Options Analysis & Risk Register Evaluated three strategic options (Do Nothing, Expand Canteen, Implement Online System), recommended the digital solution, and documented 9 project risks each with likelihood ratings and mitigation strategies.

Travel Expense Reimbursement
AS-IS Process Mapping

This project documented the complete current-state (AS-IS) workflow for employee travel expense reimbursement. Using Lucidchart, I created a structured swimlane process map exposing friction in the approval chain — forming the analytical baseline for a future TO-BE process redesign.

4 Key Deliverables
  • AS-IS Swimlane Process Map (Lucidchart) Produced a detailed swimlane diagram mapping the complete reimbursement workflow across all roles — Employee, Line Manager, Finance Team and Payroll — with every step, decision gateway and data flow labelled in BPMN notation.
  • Process Narrative & Step-by-Step Documentation Accompanied the visual map with a written narrative covering the trigger event, submission method, approval routing logic, finance review criteria and payroll processing steps for training and handover use.
  • Pain Point Register & Friction Analysis Identified and documented key AS-IS inefficiencies — ambiguous approval authority, manual form submission, lack of employee status visibility and inconsistent processing timescales — ranked by frequency and business impact.
  • Baseline & Scope Definition for TO-BE Redesign Formalised the AS-IS documentation as the analytical baseline, defining in-scope boundaries, exclusions, the process owner and the specific pain points the TO-BE redesign must address — ensuring a shared, evidence-based starting point.
